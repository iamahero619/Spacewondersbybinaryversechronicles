Galaxies: The Cosmic Cities of Stars

Explore the vast and mysterious galaxies that fill our universe.

What is a Galaxy?

A galaxy is a massive system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter, all held together by gravity. The word "galaxy" comes from the Greek word galaxias, meaning "milky," a reference to our own Milky Way galaxy.

Source-Pinterest

Types of Galaxies

Galaxies are classified into three main types based on their shape:

Source-wikipedia

Spiral Galaxies

Characterized by their stunning, pinwheel-like arms. Examples include the Milky Way and Andromeda Galaxy.

Source-wikipedia

Elliptical Galaxies

Smooth and featureless, containing mostly older stars. Example: Messier 87.

Source-wikipedia

Irregular Galaxies

Lack a distinct shape and often appear chaotic. Example: The Large Magellanic Cloud.

The Milky Way: Our Home Galaxy

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy, spanning about 100,000 light-years in diameter. It contains an estimated 100 to 400 billion stars, including our Sun.

Source-NASA

Galaxies in Motion

Galaxies are dynamic systems in constant motion. Stars orbit the galactic center, while galaxies themselves move through the universe, often interacting with one another.

Source-NASA

The Scale of Galaxies

The sheer size of galaxies is almost incomprehensible. For example, the Milky Way is so large that light takes 100,000 years to travel from one end to the other.

Source-wikipedia

Fun Facts About Galaxies

  • The oldest known galaxy, GN-z11, is located 13.4 billion light-years away.
  • The largest galaxy ever discovered, IC 1101, is 50 times the size of the Milky Way.
  • There are an estimated 2 trillion galaxies in the observable universe.
